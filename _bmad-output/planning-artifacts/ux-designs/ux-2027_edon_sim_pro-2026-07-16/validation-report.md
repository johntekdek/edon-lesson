## Overall verdict

The spine pair is a genuinely source-extractable contract: all five UJs are accounted for (UJ-4's exclusion stated three times), the 24-component vocabulary matches exactly across both files (24/24 parity, identical order), every frontmatter token carries a value and every `{path.to.token}` reference resolves, and every stated contrast ratio was verified computationally and is correct. The rubric's one high-severity internal contradiction (Flow 1 shipping the exact copy string the Voice and Tone table bans) and three medium gaps (the unseen gold dot with behavior but no visual spec, the Completion summary surface with no anatomy, no failed-save/offline state for the Authoring UI) were judged non-blocking for architecture — and none survived the day.

The two adversarial lenses shifted the picture along two themes. First, **state survival on hostile floor devices**: the low-spec/embedding review showed the spines had modeled the floor device as a featureless browser tab when the field device is a hostile one — it kills the tab, drops the POST, reloads the page — so page reload mid-lesson, quiz submission on a dying connection, token expiry mid-review, and the Moodle mobile app as a client were unspecified or held up only by a narrative sentence in a flow. Second, **text alternatives for AI-generated visual content as a schema-shaped gap**: the accessibility review found no text-alternative contract anywhere for the content that is the product's point, inside a schema advertised as playable forever (I-3) — deferring it would make early Published Versions permanently inaccessible. Every critical, high, and medium finding — 4 critical, 10 high, 15 medium across the three lenses as enumerated — and 17 of the 20 lows were applied to the spines in a same-day fix pass; the questions needing stakeholder or architecture ownership were converted to Open Items 9–17, and three rubric lows were consciously accepted as-is.
