2027_edon_sim_pro — UX Design Validation Report

_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/ux-designs/ux-2027_edon_sim_pro-2026-07-16/DESIGN.md · EXPERIENCE.md

The spine pair is a genuinely source-extractable contract: all five UJs are accounted for (UJ-4's exclusion stated three times), the 24-component vocabulary matches exactly across both files (24/24 parity, identical order), every frontmatter token carries a value and every {path.to.token} reference resolves, and every stated contrast ratio was verified computationally and is correct. The rubric's one high-severity internal contradiction (Flow 1 shipping the exact copy string the Voice and Tone table bans) and three medium gaps (the unseen gold dot with behavior but no visual spec, the Completion summary surface with no anatomy, no failed-save/offline state for the Authoring UI) were judged non-blocking for architecture — and none survived the day.

The two adversarial lenses shifted the picture along two themes. First, state survival on hostile floor devices: the low-spec/embedding review showed the spines had modeled the floor device as a featureless browser tab when the field device is a hostile one — it kills the tab, drops the POST, reloads the page — so page reload mid-lesson, quiz submission on a dying connection, token expiry mid-review, and the Moodle mobile app as a client were unspecified or held up only by a narrative sentence in a flow. Second, text alternatives for AI-generated visual content as a schema-shaped gap: the accessibility review found no text-alternative contract anywhere for the content that is the product's point, inside a schema advertised as playable forever (I-3) — deferring it would make early Published Versions permanently inaccessible. Every critical, high, and medium finding — 4 critical, 10 high, 15 medium across the three lenses as enumerated — and 17 of the 20 lows were applied to the spines in a same-day fix pass; the questions needing stakeholder or architecture ownership were converted to Open Items 9–17, and three rubric lows were consciously accepted as-is.

Accessibility review (adversarial, WCAG 2.1 AA lens)

review-accessibility.md

Reviewer lens: WCAG 2.1 AA (the spines' own recommendation for OQ-7), screen reader, keyboard/switch, low-vision/zoom, cognitive, vestibular — on Android 8-era student hardware. In the reviewer's words: the pragmatic floor is better than most MVP floors — transcripts as real text, poster fallbacks with full text alternatives, glyph+text paired with color, no autoplay, no hover-only affordances, reorder buttons beside drag; the contrast arithmetic was independently recomputed and every listed pair genuinely passes AA. But the floor was not AA-adequate as written, and its weakest points are exactly where the product's identity lives: there was no text-alternative contract anywhere for the AI-generated visual content that is the product's point — a gap baked into a schema advertised as playable forever (I-3) — and the floor's two boldest claims (simulation keyboard access "enforced via pre-publish checks", diagram "labels legible at phone width") were assertions with no mechanism behind them. The color system's own rule ("gold never carries meaning on light surfaces") was violated twice by the design's own components.

Counts as the review states them: 1 critical · 5 high · 7 medium · 4 low (eight medium findings are enumerated below — the review's own count line undercounts by one). All were applied to the spines in the same-day fix pass; the schema-field half of F-1 is escalated as Open Item 15.

critical

F-1 — No text-alternative contract for AI-generated visual content; the omission is schema-shaped and permanent

DESIGN § Components (Diagram, Poster, Model3D, chat message); EXPERIENCE § Accessibility Floor
Nothing required alt text for lesson Diagram SVGs or posters, and the allowlist-based Sanitisation would itself strip whatever accessibility markup the LLM emitted — unlabeled graphics in the one channel that bypasses the Review Gate, made permanent by Schema v1.0 immutability (I-3).
Resolution: Fixed in spine (text-alternative contract, Teacher-editable via the A-28 map extension, plus Sanitisation allowlist entries for <title>/<desc>/role="img"/aria-label(ledby)); the schema-field addition is escalated as Open Item 15 (strongly recommended before schema freeze; owner: stakeholder + Architect).
high

F-2 — Quiz instant feedback is silent to screen readers; correct-answer reveal is color/border-only

EXPERIENCE § Accessibility Floor + § Component Patterns → Quiz block
The single most frequent state change a Student experiences was absent from the aria-live inventory, and the revealed correct answer was an outline only — a direct WCAG 1.4.1 failure inside the one component the design otherwise got right.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — per-question feedback added to the live-region inventory; revealed answer gets glyph + "Correct answer" text prefix, programmatically associated.
high

F-3 — Block navigation swaps content in place with no focus management or announcement spec

EXPERIENCE § Component Patterns → Player shell; § Moodle Embedding Contract
A screen-reader user presses Next and perceives nothing — linear playback, the Player's whole job (FR-13), was effectively unusable non-visually.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — on block change focus moves to the new block's heading, "Block n of N — {title}" announced, progress header given a programmatic reading, block headings made real headings.
high

F-4 — Simulation keyboard accessibility "enforced" by checks that don't check it

EXPERIENCE § Accessibility Floor vs PRD FR-17/A-35
A-35's minimum check list contained no keyboard check — a prompt-contract line with no verification is a hope, not a floor, and LLM-generated code is precisely the code that will violate it.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — A-35 check list extended (declared parameters must map to native form controls or keyboard-operable equivalents, asserted in the check harness); native controls required in the generation contract; poster parameter-description text named as the last-resort equivalent.
high

F-5 — "Labels legible at phone width" and "pinch zoom native" are outcomes with no mechanism; the embedding contract can forbid the only recourse

EXPERIENCE § Component Patterns → Diagram block, Flow 3; § Moodle Embedding Contract
No rule produced legibility at a ~328px column, and the claimed recourse (pinch zoom) is host-controlled — a theme with user-scalable=no left a WCAG 1.4.4 failure with no owner.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — minimum rendered label size in the diagram generation/validation contract, an in-container enlarge affordance, and user-scalable=yes stated as a mod_edonlesson integration requirement.

Low-spec device floor & Moodle-embedding review (adversarial)

review-lowspec-embedding.md

Lens: a 2 GB Android 8 phone with an outdated WebView, on metered data, inside a Moodle page — and a teacher on a short-lived token. Everything below is a field scenario, not a taste note. In the reviewer's words: the spines are unusually honest about the floor — tap-to-load everywhere, poster-as-first-class, no webfonts/no shadows, feature-detection-not-UA — and that discipline is real and checkable. But the spines modeled the floor device as a featureless browser tab, and the field device is a hostile one: it kills the tab, drops the POST, reloads the page, and lies about its speech voices. Three whole classes of behavior the floor makes routine — page reload mid-lesson, quiz submission on a dying connection, and the Moodle mobile app as the client — were either unspecified or specified only by a narrative sentence in a flow. The reviewer's closing note: C2, C3, H1, H2 share a root cause — the spines specified what surfaces look like in each state but were silent about state survival (across taps, retries, reloads, expiries); one pass adding durability rules would close most of the review. That pass is exactly what was applied same-day.

Counts: 3 critical · 4 high · 4 medium · 2 low. All applied in the same-day fix pass; four escalations carried as Open Items 11, 14, 16, 17.

critical

C1 — The Moodle mobile app is nowhere in either spine — and it is how many students will open the course

EXPERIENCE § Moodle Embedding Contract; § Foundation; § IA
The embedding contract was written exclusively for a browser rendering the activity page; the app path was unspecified for both Student activity and Teacher launch ("what if there is no tab?"). Best case an unstyled hand-off violating the never-look-broken rule; worst case an app WebView where the Player half-works and no one designed the half.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — client-contexts rows added to the embedding contract with a designed, content-styled browser hand-off state ("This lesson opens in your browser.") for both Student and Teacher entry; app-native scope escalated as Open Item 14.
critical

C2 — Quiz submission on flaky connectivity: no double-submit protection, no durability rule — and the copy made a promise nothing guaranteed

EXPERIENCE § Component Patterns → Quiz block; § State Patterns
A double-tap on a 2G-grade connection (the universal reflex) could burn a Teacher-limited attempt; a close during "Saving your score…" could lose the submission, violating A-13; "We'll keep saving your score" was only true if durability was a rule, and it wasn't.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — Submit is single-fire, "Saving your score…" may only appear after durable acceptance, an attempt is consumed at most once per Submit; the durability mechanism escalated as Open Item 17 (owner: Architect).
critical

C3 — Page reload is the floor device's default behavior, and the spine had no resume state at all; version pinning made reload silently swap the lesson

EXPERIENCE § State Patterns → Mid-lesson connectivity loss / Version pinning
Reload is the modal session shape on the floor, not an edge; a student on Block 7 of v1 could reload into v2 — different blocks, different quiz — with no version chrome and possibly an attempt consumed. The word "silently" was a designed-in confusion on exactly the devices that reload the most.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — "Player reloaded mid-lesson" experience-floor row added (viewed-marks and submitted-quiz states visibly restored or an honest designed state, never a silent restart); "silently" deleted and "Your lecturer updated this lesson" copy added; reload-does-not-end-session/consume-attempt tied into Open Item 11.
high

H1 — "Work persisted" through token expiry was a narrative claim in Flow 1, not a rule anywhere

EXPERIENCE § Key Flows → Flow 1; § Interaction Primitives; § State Patterns
Autosave requires a live token; a token dying at minute 40 of a 90-minute review meant every subsequent autosave failed with no defined state — Flow 1's "work persisted" was asserted by the story and guaranteed by nothing. Exactly the kind of claim that dies in implementation.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — behavioral rule in the Block editor pattern (no acknowledged edit ever lost to expiry; failed autosave surfaces immediately; editor content preserved locally and restored on relaunch into the same Draft) plus an autosave-failing state row; token lifetime and expiry mechanics escalated as Open Item 16 (owner: Architect, FR-29 ADR).
high

H2 — Sequential heavy blocks: no unload policy, no return-state rule — memory pressure was the one floor constraint the ladder ignored

EXPERIENCE § Capability Tiers & Degradation Ladder; § Component Patterns → Player shell / Model3D / Simulation
Two live heavy blocks is a tab crash on 1.5–2 GB RAM — which lands you in C3 with everything lost; and re-tapping a poster raised an unanswered data-honesty question about re-downloading 4.2 MB on metered data.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — ladder rule added: at most one heavy Block live at a time, leaving releases it back to poster state, cached re-load never re-charges the student's data (or the size label honestly reappears).
high

H3 — Narration handled "no voices" but not voices that lie or voices that are wrong; the tier table had no runtime-failure row for it

EXPERIENCE § Capability Tiers (Narration column); § Component Patterns → Narration control
Android 8 WebViews routinely report voices and then speak() silently does nothing — the control would render, the student would tap, and nothing would happen: worse than the spine's own banned unlabeled spinner. "Usable voices" was doing all the work and was never defined.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — narration runtime-failure rung mirroring the Simulation one (no audible start within a bounded time → quiet conversion to the Floor state), "usable" defined behaviorally, voice preference follows the Lesson Script language tag (en-NG → en-GB → any en) with "Show text" always one tap away.
high

H4 — The Low-spec view simulated capability, not speed — the teacher's trust in the Review Gate was built on a fast laptop

EXPERIENCE § Component Patterns → Preview overlay; § Capability Tiers; § Responsive & Platform
"The Teacher sees what the Floor sees" was only half-true — the damage on real hardware is temporal, not visual — and the perceived-performance rules were directional but unmeasurable, with the PRD's CI low-spec profile (NFR-2) sitting right there as the measurement rig.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — honesty line added to the Preview overlay spec and UI copy ("what these devices see, not how fast they see it"); perceived-performance floors bound to the CI low-spec profile (first-text target, skeleton timing), CI-enforced like the bundle budget.
medium

M1 — Height self-management was asserted; layout shift was not addressed — the Next button was a moving target

EXPERIENCE § Moodle Embedding Contract; § Component Patterns → Player shell
In-flow bottom nav jumped on every Back/Next; tap-to-load swapped a poster for a taller viewer seconds after the tap, when the thumb was already resting where Next used to be.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — two contract rules: the Player brings its own top edge into view on navigation; media containers reserve their space so loading never shifts text on screen.
medium

M2 — Tap-to-load was the data-honesty headline, but posters auto-loaded with no size discipline anywhere

EXPERIENCE § Capability Tiers; DESIGN § Components → Poster fallback card; PRD FR-18
The poster path is the universal rendering path by the spine's own design — which made its weight the single biggest uncontrolled data cost in the Player.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — per-poster byte budget (value architecture-owned, CI-enforced) and lazy loading as posters' blocks approach, added to the ladder rules.
medium

M3 — Retake across Published Versions: "highest attempt" and "attempts remaining" had no student-visible story when the quiz changed

EXPERIENCE § State Patterns → Version pinning / Attempts exhausted; § Component Patterns → Quiz block
6/10 on v1, 7/8 on v2 — which is "highest"? The "highest across different denominators" question was nobody's.
Resolution: Fixed in spine — Completion summary cross-version display rule (this attempt's score plus "Your best score so far"), attempts stated per-lesson; the scoring semantics forced into Open Item 11 (owner: architecture + stakeholder).
medium
