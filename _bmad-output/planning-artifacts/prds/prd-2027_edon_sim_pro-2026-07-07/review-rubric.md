# PRD Quality Review — e-DON Lesson Studio

Reviewed: `prd.md` + `addendum.md` (prd-2027_edon_sim_pro-2026-07-07), judged against the PRD Quality Rubric. Stakes: launch. Shape: chain-top Level 3 PRD feeding UX → architecture → epics/stories, scope-fenced to the product brief §5–§7 with `project-context.md` authoritative for build rules. Source fidelity was audited separately and is not re-litigated here.

## Overall verdict

This is a decision-ready, unusually disciplined PRD: the four Load-Bearing Invariants (§1.1) act as a real thesis that every FR traces back to, all 27 FRs carry genuinely testable consequences, and the assumption/OQ hygiene (18 indexed assumptions, 10 real open questions, roundtrip-clean index) is exemplary for launch stakes. What's at risk is narrow but real: the low-spec-device promise — the headline of the primary consumer journey — has its bounds defined nowhere in the chain yet (only the mechanism), and two trade-offs are quietly unsurfaced (client-asserted quiz scores feeding the gradebook; no quality signal on the one content channel that bypasses the Review Gate). Nothing is broken; the findings below are sharpening, not rescue.

## Decision-readiness — strong

Decisions are stated as decisions, not considerations. §1.1's invariants are explicitly "non-negotiable" with a named change-control path ("requires explicit stakeholder confirmation"). Trade-offs mostly name what was given up: FR-14 commits to browser SpeechSynthesis and names neural TTS as the deferred alternative behind a provider seam; §11 names the simulation free-code vs. template-library fallback with a decision point (OQ-5); §8's Non-Goals carry the brief's "the PM must not promote these into MVP" instruction verbatim. The Open Questions are actually open — OQ-3 ("Commit to MVP or defer?"), OQ-7 (accessibility as an explicit scope *addition*), OQ-8 (NDPR posture) have no smuggled answers. `[NOTE FOR PM]` callouts sit at real tensions (FR-10's uncommitted Block-level Regeneration; §8's offline/SCORM "designed-for" boundary), not safe checkpoints. Even the counter-metrics are honestly tagged as a PM addition to "confirm or strike" (A-17) rather than presented as brief-sourced.

One objection a pushback reader would find dodged rather than acknowledged:

### Findings
- **medium** Client-side scoring integrity trade-off unsurfaced (§4.4 FR-15, §10 SM-3, §11) — FR-15 commits that "Scoring happens client-side against the Published Version," which means defined answers ship to the Student's device and the grade report to Moodle (FR-23) is client-asserted — while SM-3 makes gradebook writeback a 100% headline metric and the Student JTBD promises "know my scores count." The Risks section (§11) never names the answer-extraction / score-forgery exposure, so a stakeholder who cares about grade integrity finds their objection dodged. The mechanism is brief-fenced, so this is not a scope change — it is an unacknowledged consequence of one. *Fix:* add a §11 risk entry naming the exposure, and either an `[ASSUMPTION]` that MVP quizzes are formative-stakes (accepted risk) or an OQ on whether score reports need server-side plausibility checks downstream.

## Substance over theater — strong

No furniture found. §2 uses JTBD framing instead of persona theater; all four roles drive requirements (Teacher → Review Gate and plain-fields Authoring UI; Student → low-spec budgets, FR-18; administrators → the deliberately minimal "event logging sufficient... no dashboard"; Operator → all of Feature 4.7). UJ persona details are invented but tagged (A-1), which is the honest way to do it. The Vision (§1) could not swap into another PRD — "~60 Nigerian Colleges of Education," generate-once economics, and the Moodle/RAG substrate are load-bearing in the text itself. NFRs are product-specific: NFR-1 carries the 5-minute number, NFR-3 lists concrete mechanisms (allowlist Sanitisation, iframe sandbox + CSP, no wildcard CORS, key rotation from day one), NFR-7 names the actual clean-room constraint (OpenMAIC AGPL-3.0). The only NFR that leans on an adjective ("usable," NFR-2) is treated under Done-ness below. No findings.

## Strategic coherence — strong

The PRD has a thesis and knows it: generate-once economics plus a teacher-in-the-loop quality gate turn an institution's corpus into interactive lessons at unit economics that hold across large populations (§1, crystallized as I-1–I-4). Every feature group cites which invariant and UJ it serves; §4.5 even explains *why* Sanitisation is absolute ("the only exception to the Review Gate... which is why Sanitisation is mandatory and absolute"). The SMs validate the thesis rather than measuring activity: SM-2 is explicitly a generation-quality proxy with its logic stated inline, SM-4 measures the economic claim from telemetry alone, and the counter-metric set is genuinely adversarial — SM-C1 catches a rubber-stamped Review Gate, SM-C2 catches gaming SM-2 with slide-only lessons, SM-C3 catches cost control by throttling students. MVP scope kind is coherent (platform + problem-solving), and §9.2's deferral horizons match the V3 arc.

### Findings
- **low** No adoption or usage metric (§10) — the SM set validates the supply side (speed, quality proxy, cost, integration) but nothing measures whether Teachers adopt the tool or Students complete lessons, even though §2.1 promises administrators "evidence of usage and outcomes" and FR-27 logs exactly the events needed. Targets are brief-fenced (§4 of the brief), so this is an OQ candidate, not a silent addition. *Fix:* raise as an OQ (e.g., "should an adoption/completion SM be added from Structured Events?") for stakeholder confirmation.

## Done-ness clarity — strong

This is the PRD's best craft. Every FR ships a "Consequences (testable)" block, and they are real acceptance criteria, not restatements: FR-3 ("A non-conforming pipeline output is never stored as a Draft... never a silent gap"), FR-12 ("no Student-accessible path to Drafts exists"), FR-20 ("an SVG that cannot be made conforming is rejected outright"), FR-27 (the zero-cost cache-hit telemetry carve-out so SM-5 stays computable). Where "gracefully" appears, it is immediately defined by its consequences (FR-2: renders other Blocks, completes without error; FR-14: lesson completable, narration text accessible) — the adjective is decoration on a bound, which passes. Deferred values are deferred *explicitly* with a named owner ("values set in architecture," "exact normalisation is an architecture decision") rather than left vague. Two residual gaps, both at the boundary of what the PRD delegated:

### Findings
- **medium** The low-spec performance bound exists nowhere in the chain (§4.4 FR-18, §5 NFR-2) — "performs acceptably on low-spec Android devices" is the one load-bearing adjective left standing. The PRD delegates values to architecture, and `project-context.md` §7 supplies only the *mechanism* ("low-spec profile (throttled CPU, constrained memory) in CI," per-block size budgets) — no reference device class, no budget numbers, no interaction bound anywhere. FR-18 is the spine of UJ-2, the primary consumer journey; an engineer today cannot say what "done" looks like for it, and FR-16's "orbit and zoom work on supported devices" inherits the same undefined term. Delegation is legitimate; an unbounded delegation on the headline consumer promise is not. *Fix:* add one measurable anchor in the PRD (e.g., a reference device class or "lesson start-to-first-Slide under N seconds on the CI throttle profile") or an OQ explicitly requiring architecture to set the numbers before story creation.
- **medium** "Accurate" diagrams have no done-ness or feedback loop (§1 Vision, §4.5 FR-19) — the Vision and FR-19 promise "accurate labelled diagrams," but the consequences cover only grounding and rendering. Diagram Requests are the sole channel bypassing the Review Gate (I-2), so no human ever checks accuracy, and no SM or Structured Event measures it — SM-5 measures cost, SM-C3 measures denials. If diagrams are confidently wrong, nothing in this PRD would detect it. *Fix:* either add a lightweight quality signal (a Student "report diagram" action logged as a Structured Event) or an explicit `[ASSUMPTION]` that Corpus grounding is accepted as the sole accuracy proxy for MVP — confirm with stakeholder.
- **low** NFR-8 is an adjective without a consequence (§5) — "localisation-ready text handling" is the only NFR with no testable form. *Fix:* one consequence, e.g., "no user-facing string is hardcoded in code; Lesson Script content carries a language tag."

## Scope honesty — strong

Exemplary. §8 does real work: nine explicit non-goals sourced to brief §7, plus three derived "by invariant" exclusions that would otherwise be silently assumed. `[NON-GOAL for MVP]` tags appear inline exactly where a reader might infer scope (FR-2's V3 Block types, FR-27's dashboard). All 18 assumptions are tagged where they occur and indexed in §13 with cross-links to the OQs that resolve them (A-3→OQ-9, A-6→OQ-10, A-10→OQ-4, A-15→OQ-8). De-scoping is proposed, never done silently — FR-10 carries Block-level Regeneration as "uncommitted" with a `[NOTE FOR PM]` rather than quietly dropping or including it.

On open-items density: 18 assumptions + 10 OQs on a launch-stakes PRD warrants scrutiny per the rubric. It passes: the assumptions are overwhelmingly low-risk semantic pins (session refresh semantics, retry mechanics, flag granularity) that *narrow* ambiguity rather than paper over unknowns, and the OQs partition cleanly into value-setting (OQ-1, OQ-2, OQ-6), semantics confirmation (OQ-4, OQ-9, OQ-10), and genuine scope decisions (OQ-3, OQ-5, OQ-7, OQ-8). This is honesty, not indecision. One sharpening:

### Findings
- **low** Scope-shaping OQs lack a resolution point (§12) — OQ-3 (Block Regeneration), OQ-5 (simulation mode), OQ-7 (accessibility), and OQ-8 (data protection) change what gets built, but nothing says which OQs must close before epics/story creation versus which can ride into architecture. Only OQ-1 names an owner. *Fix:* mark each OQ with "resolve by" (before architecture / before epics / during build) so downstream workflows know what blocks them.

## Downstream usability — strong

This PRD was built to be source-extracted. The Glossary (§3) opens with an explicit contract ("Downstream workflows must use these terms exactly") and the FRs honor it — Tenant, Draft, Published Version, Generation Job, Sanitisation are used with disciplined capitalization throughout. IDs are contiguous and unique (FR-1–27, UJ-1–5, SM-1–5 + SM-C1–C3, OQ-1–10, A-1–18); every FR↔OQ and SM↔FR cross-reference I traced resolves (FR-6→OQ-10, FR-10→OQ-3, FR-15→OQ-4, FR-26→OQ-9, SM-4→OQ-1, SM-5→OQ-2, §11→OQ-5). Each feature group states which UJs it realizes, so sections lift out cleanly. The Assumptions Index roundtrips perfectly — every inline `[ASSUMPTION]` is indexed and every index entry appears inline (verified all 18). The addendum is a model of chain-top routing: architect, SM, and UX each get their preserved depth with authority explicitly ceded to `project-context.md`. Findings are mechanical-grade only (see Mechanical notes).

## Shape fit — strong

Right shape, consciously chosen. Multi-stakeholder B2B with meaningful UX → UJs with protagonists are load-bearing and present (UJ-1/UJ-5 carry the authoring arc, UJ-2 the consumer arc, each with edge cases that became FRs — UJ-2's degradation edge is FR-18, UJ-4's budget-exhaustion edge is FR-26/OQ-9). The Operator surface is deliberately *not* over-formalized (A-2: "minimal internal tooling, not a polished admin product") — correct calibration for a single-operator role rather than UJ inflation. Chain-top obligations are taken seriously: §0 states the document's position in the workflow, the addendum routes downstream depth, and the schema-first framing (§4.1 "keystone") gives architecture its anchor. No shape forcing detected. No findings.
