Calculating current with V = IR

I = V ÷ R

Ohm’s Law links three quantities: voltage, current and resistance. When a 12 volt battery drives a 4 ohm resistor, the current is 12 divided by 4 — three amperes.

Whenever you know two of the three, you can find the third.

1. A 12 V battery is connected across a 4 Ω resistor. What current flows through the circuit?

2. The voltage stays the same and the resistance doubles. What happens to the current?

Not quite. With the same voltage, doubling the resistance halves the current.
